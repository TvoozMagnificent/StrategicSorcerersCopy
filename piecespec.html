Piece Specifications in Strategic Sorcerers

Starting Position: The pawns are placed on the second rank for White and seventh rank for Black.
Movement:
Pawns move forward but capture diagonally.
On their first move, pawns have the option to move two squares forward.
Pawns cannot move backward.
Special Rules:
En Passant: A pawn capturing rule which allows a pawn to capture an opponent's pawn in a specific situation.
Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opponent's back rank, it can be promoted to any other piece (except another king).
Starting Position: In each corner of the board (a1, h1 for White; a8, h8 for Black).
Movement:
Rooks move horizontally or vertically along ranks and files.
They can move any number of squares in a straight line.
Rooks cannot jump over other pieces.
Special Rules: None.
Starting Position: Next to each rook (b1, g1 for White; b8, g8 for Black).
Movement:
Knights move in an L-shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular to that.
Knights are the only pieces that can "jump" over other pieces.
Special Rules: None.

Starting Position: Next to each knight (c1, f1 for White; c8, f8 for Black).
Movement:
Bishops move diagonally along their color (light or dark) squares.
They can move any number of squares diagonally.
Special Rules: None.

Starting Position: The Queen starts on the remaining square of her color (d1 for White; d8 for Black).
Movement:
Queens combine the movements of rooks and bishops. They can move horizontally, vertically, or diagonally any number of squares.
Special Rules: None.

Starting Position: The King is placed in the center square of the back rank (e1 for White; e8 for Black).
Movement:
The King can move one square in any direction: horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
Special Rules:
Castling: A special move involving the King and either rook under certain conditions.
Check and Checkmate: Special conditions related to the safety of the King.

Description: A special move involving the King and one of the rooks.
Conditions:
The King and the involved rook have not moved.
There are no pieces between the King and the rook.
The King is not in check, and it cannot move through or onto a square that is attacked by an opponent’s piece.
The King cannot be in check after the castling move.
